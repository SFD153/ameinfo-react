Leading the Future with Disruptive Innovation

Today, the digital revolution has exposed abundant value opportunities for companies to raise efficiency and develop new kinds of products, services, and business models. A small group of outstanding players across industries and economies has shown that large incumbents can innovate, release trapped value, compete with disruptors—and even act as disruptors. These companies are executing what we call a “wise pivot”—a way to reinvent themselves and harness the power of disruption. They are using data, digital tools, and new ways of working to optimize and grow the core business, even as they build a future business.

Accenture’s Innovation Summit 2019 will bring together some of the best talents and leaders in Saudi Arabia to discuss some of the best discussions on innovation such as trapped value, risk, and the latest technology trends in Saudi Arabia.

Join us on April 24, at Accenture Innovation Summit 2019 to see how leaders can act now to take their digital transformation to the next level, shape new technology and innovation strategies and set a new series of endgames in their sights.

Xavi Anglada brings with him over 22 years of experience in strategy, consulting and operations in the FinTech, Media, and Telecommunication industries across Europe, Asia, Africa and the Middle East.

Dr. Sulaiman A. Mirdad

Adviser at Communication and Information Technology Commission

Read More

Dr. Sulaiman A. Mirdad

Dr. Sulaiman Mirdad has 30+ years of professional experience in the US and the Middle East, he was an Advisor to the Minister of Communication and Information Technology (MCIT) responsible for IT industry development. Prior to that he was the Deputy Governor for Information Technology at the Communications and Information Technology Commission (CITC), Saudi Arabia. Prior to Joining CITC, Dr. Mirdad was VP for Technology at the Advanced Arabian Systems (Naseej), and Cofounder and Principal Engineer at InfoLibria, a provider of Internet solutions for content distribution and delivery products. With more than 25 years of experience in the field of IT, Dr. Mirdad coauthored a number of worldwide patents, while working at InfoLibria, in the subject of Content Distribution and Delivery. For his achievements, Dr. Mirdad was granted the King Abdulaziz Order of Merit (First Degree) in 2008 by the Custodian of the Two Holy Mosques King Abdullah Bin Abdulaziz.

Dr. Mirdad has a Ph.D. in Computer Science from Boston University. His patented research was one of the first to propose the integration of application and communications layers (known today as Layered Switching), and integration of routing and caching (transparency between networks and applications). He was the first to introduce the concept of Transparent Proxies. Dr. Mirdad received an M.S. in Computer Science from The American University at Washington D.C. and a B.S. in Computer Engineering from King Fahad University of Petroleum & Minerals.
